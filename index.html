About

My name is Kwan Holloway and I'm a Multimedia Developer. I mainly focus on Game Design, Game Programming, and Web Development. I'm an Honors senior at Brooklyn College and a lover of technology.

I'm interested in Gaming and Graphic Design. I take narratological and educational approaches to gaming, wanting my games to immerse players in deep story as well as teach them how to do new things.

I'm also a Peer Mentor at Brooklyn College, helping incoming first-years gain their bearings.

I'm a creative guy who has tons of interesting ideas in my mind. I'm also a big gamer, loving game series like Kingdom Hearts, Metal Gear, and Final Fantasy. Games have changed me as a person and allowed me to grow. I want to give back to people by touching their lives with my games.

I want my games to contain complex and meaningful stories that bring about introspection and change in people, all the while being exceptionally fun! More than that, games can also educate people and make the learning process easier, and more engaging. During the Spring of 2016 and the Fall of 2016, I've worked in small teams to develop 2 educational games. The first revolves around teaching music, and the second focuses on teaching GRE vocab.

I love programming and the challenge it provides. I love writing code, but I also enjoy getting stuck and having to solve logical puzzles in order to get my code working. The ability to overcome obstacles is something I value highly in myself, and this trait can be partially attributed to my studies in the art of Parkour and its philosophy. In Parkour, there is always a way to beat an obstacle, it's just a matter of how creative you can get with the tools you have, for example, your body in this case.
